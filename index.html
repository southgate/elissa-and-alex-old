Travel Guide

Palm Springs is a fun place to explore. We hope that you get a little extra time to find an adventure or something tasty. We've collected a few suggestions of our favorite things to eat, see, and do.

Food & Drink

King's Highway

Right in front of Ace Hotel, King's Highway is a former Denny's converted into a bohemian roadside diner serving California standards, and Stumptown coffee. Great for Breakfast or Lunch, make sure you visit at least once while you're here. We'll be here for Sunday brunch.

Cheeky's

Home to one of the best Eggs Benedict in America, a fried chicken sandwich to die for, and for the bacon lovers: a house-cured bacon flight. You'll want a nap (preferably pool-side) afterwards. This place is popular and they don't take reservations so be prepared to wait.

Lappert's

This Hawaiian-style ice cream shop is run by the son of the famous Lappert's of Kauai. is a perfect excuse to cool down on a hot desert day. Try an avocado ice cream, a shave ice, or our favorite: a Dole Whip. Seriously, you haven't lived until you've had a Dole Whip.

Adventure

Aerial Tramway

The worlds largest rotating tram car takes you on a 2.5 mile path up above 8,500 feet to Mountain Station for a stunning view of the valley below, and miles of hiking trails. The peak is about 30 degrees colder than downtown Palm Springs. Buy tickets ahead of time.

Scoot Palm Springs

Rent a vespa-style scooter and go explore the city! Scoot Palm Springs works with the Ace Hotel and has a few scooters on site, so just head to the front desk to get set up. California residents need a motorcycle license, but any license will do for out-of-staters.

Joshua Tree National Park

This massive national park is where the Mojave and Colorado deserts meet. A wild landscape that can feel like another planet, it gets its name from a tree that's right out of a Dr. Seuss book. A great place to hike, rock climb, and camp. Plan to spend a whole day here, it takes over an hour to drive to either entrance.

Art & Architecture

Palm Springs Art Museum

This museum hosts a good collection of contemporary and historic works, however the sculpture garden is particularly great. The desert-modern concrete building itself is worth the visit. Also don't miss the Architecture and Design center, a separate building made of glass with awesome views.

Palm Springs Modern Tours

Palm Springs is one of the world's largest and best-preserved concentrations of modernist architecture. This tour guide organizes small groups (6 max) to learn more about the city and some of the more distinctive homes and buildings. Call ahead to schedule.

Palm Springs Windmill Tours

The iconic windmill farm just outside Palm Springs is quite a sight. If you drove in from L.A. you might have seen them, otherwise these are worth a visit. Go on your own or schedule a tour to learn more.

Shopping

Palm Springs Galleria

A cluster of small design shops and art galleries has a lot to see and might be a good place to start if you're looking for a souvenir.

SHAG

Midcentury illustrations, artwork, and funky furniture and props, this place is bright and colorful.

Modernway

Midcentury furniture, audio, and other items. We've never actually bought anything here, but it sure is a fun place to browse.

What To Wear

Desert Formal Attire

Ladies

Friday's formal dinner is our fancy night, so class up the joint in your favorite color. Stay cool (and cool) in flowy, formal maxi dresses. It can get cool at night, so bring a sweater, shawl, scarf or other warmth-cocoon.

Gents

For Friday's formal dinner, look sharp. Wear a suit and tie (or bowtie?) in whatever color you look best in (it doesn't have to be dark). A cotton or linen suit will be more comfortable than wool in the heat.

Festive Attire

Ladies

Saturday's ceremony is party time! Wear something festive that will take you comfortably from an outdoor ceremony to the dance floor. It gets cool when the sun goes down, so plan accordingly.

Gents

For Saturday's ceremony, have fun and look good. Try a suit in a bright color or a fun patterned shirt. Look to your socks or pocket square for complimenting colors and patterns. Tie optional.
